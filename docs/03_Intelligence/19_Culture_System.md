# 19_Culture_System

This document specifies cultural transmission, technological traditions, and memory synchronization.

---

## 1. Cultural Transmission Protocols
Culture is defined as shared knowledge and behavioral biases passed non-genetically:
* **Horizontal Transmission:** Same-generation shapes sharing coordinate memories via social interactions.
* **Vertical Transmission:** Parents mentoring offspring during the infant phase, transmitting skill boosts.
* **Oblique Transmission:** Sentinels broadcasting policies and tech blueprints.

---

## 2. Technology Traditions
Different colonies develop distinct tech traditions based on local resource scarcity:
* **Desert Colonies:** Focus research on solar energy collectors and mining efficiency.
* **Forest Colonies:** Focus research on food yield and wood construction.

---

## 3. Cultural Drift
When colonies are separated by large spatial distances, their tech databases drift apart. When two isolated colonies rejoin, they exchange tech branches via Sentinel-to-Sentinel database syncs.
